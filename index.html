The Curse of the Hollowshade Hill

Welcome to a land drowning in rot and superstition. This Daggerheart one-shot, designed for five Tier 2, Level 2 characters, plunges the heroes into a cursed swamp where ancient evils stir. They will navigate a gauntlet of macabre folklore, monstrous spiders, and manipulative crones to confront a horror festering in the dark. This adventure is a tense crawl through a dying land, where heroism is measured by the grim choices made when all options seem bleak. Prepare for a tale of heroic fantasy steeped in horror.

The Setting

Hollowshade is a vast, swampy mangrove forest that swallowed an ancient battlefield whole. Constant rains and a diverted river turned the war-torn land into a stagnant mire dotted with tiny, isolated villages. At its heart looms Hollowshade Hill, the centerpiece of the region's dark legends. It is said that atop this hill, a coven of witches known as the "Ladies of the Forest" defeated and imprisoned a powerful druid. From this prison, a monstrous, gnarled tree sprouted, growing so vast its blood-red leaves cast the entire hill in perpetual shadow. The locals believe the witches rule this domain, keeping the worst of its evils in check. If only that were true.

The Threat

An ancient, alien consciousness—a being of parasitic rot and arachnoid hunger—is stirring in its prison beneath Hollowshade Hill. This is the true source of the curse, the druid's prison merely the vessel it has corrupted over centuries. It now influences the swamp's fauna, driving beasts mad and breeding monstrous spiders that serve as its eyes and claws. It whispers in dreams, promising power to the desperate, like the witches who unknowingly draw their strength from its corruption. If it breaks free, its influence will spread like a plague, transforming the entire region into a writhing nest of horrors.

Recent Developments

The imprisoned entity's power is growing. Its influence seeps into the swamp, causing the wildlife to become more aggressive and monstrous spiders to wander further from the hill. The "Ladies of the Forest," sensing a shift in the balance of power, have become more active, their demands on the local populace growing crueler as their own tainted magic wavers. They treat Hollowshade as their domain, blind to the fact that they are merely caretakers for a far greater evil—one that now sees them as pests in its own nest.

Adventure Summary

Forced by an urgent quest to take the fastest route, a party of five Tier 2, Level 2 heroes must traverse the cursed Hollowshade swamp. They are quickly embroiled in the locals' grim traditions, which are dedicated to the manipulative "Ladies of the Forest." A shared, prophetic nightmare reveals a greater threat: a monstrous, beating heart beneath the gnarled tree on Hollowshade Hill. Tasked by the suspicious village Ealdorman, the heroes investigate the hill, battling frenzied beasts and spider-horrors to uncover the truth. They learn they must bargain with the witches, who demand a bloody price to help them destroy the entity, forcing the heroes into a grim alliance to confront the Chittering Heart and decide the fate of Hollowshade.

Adventure Hooks

1. The Heartseed

You are entrusted with a faintly glowing seed that is the only cure for a beloved noble, mentor, or family member who is dying from a magical plague. The seed must be planted in a sanctum on the far side of Hollowshade within the week, or it will wither and your charge will die. The path through the swamp is the only one short enough.

2. The Lost Patrol

A fellow Daggerheart, a friend or rival, led a small patrol into Hollowshade a fortnight ago to investigate strange reports and never returned. You carry a token they gave you. You have tracked them to the edge of the swamp, and now you must venture in to find out what happened and bring them home.

3. The Witch's Debt

One of your ancestors made a pact with the Ladies of the Forest, and that debt is now due. A spectral messenger has delivered a summons: come to Hollowshade and serve the Ladies, or the ancient curse will fall upon your own family. You do not know the nature of the service, only that to refuse is to invite ruin.
