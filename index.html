The Curse of the Hollowshade Hill

An interactive guide for the Game Master

Overview

"A group of heroes on an urgent mission must detour through a cursed swamp, only to discover that the local legends of cruel witches hide a terrifying and ancient evil that is about to break free."

Adventure At-a-Glance

Beat Est. Time Key Events
1: The Wary Outpost 45 Mins Arrive in a remote village; learn unsettling rumors about Hollowshade.
2: The Trail of Treats 45 Mins Encounter desperate children and the macabre nature of the witches' "gifts."
Intermission: A Pounding Dream 5 Mins Experience a shared, prophetic dream foreshadowing the evil at the hill's heart.
3: The Silk-Strangled Den 60 Mins Ascend the web-choked hill; survive an ambush by monstrous spiders.
4: The Witches' Council 45 Mins Confront the "Ladies of the Forest"; learn the truth of the druid's prison.
5: Into the Gnarled Heart 30 Mins Enter the corrupted heart-tree at the hill's summit.
6: The Chittering Heart 60 Mins Battle the monstrous source of the curse in its subterranean lair.
Beat 1: The Wary Outpost

Setup

The adventure begins in Mire's End, a remote village at the edge of the Hollowshade mangroves. The party is on an urgent quest, carrying a Heartseed—a faintly glowing magical seed that must be planted in a sanctum beyond the forest to save a dying noble. The perilous path through Hollowshade is the only one fast enough.

The track dissolves into a muddy street that cuts through a collection of sagging, moss-covered buildings. This must be Mire's End. A perpetual drizzle falls from a grey sky, and the air hangs thick with the smell of wet earth and woodsmoke. A single tavern, its sign depicting a boot half-sunk in mud, offers the only sign of welcome. You clutch the pouch containing the Heartseed; you can feel its faint, desperate pulse against your palm. Time is running out.

GM Notes

Stakes: The life of a noble depends on the party delivering the Heartseed in time. Delaying means the noble dies. Heading into Hollowshade unprepared could mean the party dies with them.

Mechanics: Dynamic Progress Countdown (4): Uncovering the Truth

The party advances the countdown by successfully gathering information. When it reaches 0, they have learned enough to proceed with a clear understanding of the immediate dangers.

Clues:
  • Core Clue: The villagers of Hollowshade are a "hardened and weird bunch," who offer prayers to witches they call the "Ladies of the Forest."
  • Core Clue: Hollowshade Hill is a place of active danger. A great, twisted tree that blots out the sun stands at its peak, swarming with monstrous spiders and frenzied beasts.
  • Flex Clue (from a trapper named Silas): "Ladies of the Forest? Complete and utter nonsense. Just old crones in the wood. It's the *spiders* you need to worry about." He can show a nasty, web-scarred bite mark on his arm.
  • Flex Clue (from a nervous merchant): He speaks of the Hollowshade locals sending their children on a "Trail of Treats" into the forest as tribute. The thought clearly terrifies him.
Checks:
  • Presence (Charm) check DC 14: To coax stories from the tight-lipped locals.
  • Instinct (Sense) check DC 14: To determine who is speaking from genuine experience versus who is repeating gossip.
  • Knowledge (Recall) check DC 14: To see if any character has read of the "Ladies of the Forest" or legends of a druid's prison in regional folklore.
Escalations (Fear Spends):
  • Spend 1 Fear: A villager overhears the party's questions and becomes hostile, accusing them of being witch-sympathizers. This imposes disadvantage on the next social check.
  • Spend 1 Fear: A man bursts into the tavern, covered in mud and blood, raving about his hunting hounds turning on him near Hollowshade, their eyes glowing with a sickly green light.
Beat 2: The Trail of Treats

Setup

Leaving Mire's End, the party enters the cursed lands of Hollowshade. The path is a muddy track cutting through dark, gnarled mangrove-like trees dripping with moss and strange webs. The colossal, blood-leafed tree on the distant hill is now visible.

The air grows still and heavy, thick with the scent of decay and damp earth. The trees here are twisted and menacing, their branches clawing at the sky. Ahead, two small figures, a boy and a slightly older girl, trudge down the muddy path. The girl clutches a small, empty wooden pail. They seem impossibly small in the oppressive gloom of the forest.

GM Notes

Stakes: The immediate safety of the two children, Elspeth (8) and Finn (6). Their story presents a moral quandary about the desperation of the Hollowshade folk.

Mechanics: Dynamic Consequence Countdown (3): The Children's Hope Fades

If the party is cruel or hesitates, use this countdown. If it reaches 0, Finn breaks down crying, refusing to move.

Dialogue & Clues:
  • Elspeth: *"I spilled the milk. All of it. Papa got... quiet. He told Mama the Ladies would provide. He sent us on the Trail of Treats."*
  • Finn: *"The trail has sweets! The Ladies leave them for children who are sorry."*
  • A short distance down the path are the "treats": crude gingerbread men and candies stuck to trees.
  • The Reveal: If a character interacts with a treat, it dissolves into a writhing fistful of maggots. This reveals the "gifts" are a horrifying illusion.
Checks:
  • Presence (Charm) check DC 14: To gently earn the children's trust and get them to speak openly.
  • Instinct (Sense) check DC 14: To notice the foul magic on a "treat" before touching it.
Escalations (Fear Spends):
  • Spend 1 Fear: A frenzied, sickly-looking hybrid animal (a small, chittering creature with too many legs) darts from the trees, hissing at the children before vanishing.
  • Spend 1 Fear: As a character inspects a gingerbread man, its features briefly twist into a screaming face before dissolving, forcing an Instinct reaction roll (DC 14) to avoid marking a Stress.
Intermission: A Pounding Dream

Setup

This event occurs the next time the party takes a long rest in Hollowshade. It is a shared nightmare.

Sleep does not bring rest. You stand together in a field of grey, dead grass under a bruised-purple sky. A sickly full moon hangs overhead, and in the distance, the colossal tree from the hill claws at the sky, its leaves the color of dried blood. A sound, a feeling more than a sound, fills you: a slow, rhythmic *thump-thump... thump-thump...* like a monstrous heart beating deep within the earth. Then, a voice echoes in your minds—a desperate, fractured whisper. *"Free me... please... free me..."*

GM Notes

Stakes: The dream introduces a supernatural mystery. Is the voice a victim begging for release, or the source of evil luring them into a trap?

Checks:
  • Knowledge (Recall) check DC 15: A success confirms the tree from the dream matches legends of the one on Hollowshade Hill, said to be the prison of an ancient druid.
Beat 3: The Silk-Strangled Den

Setup

The party begins their ascent of Hollowshade Hill. The ground rises from the swamp into a steep incline, now completely under the shadow of the great, blood-leafed tree. Every surface is coated in thick, sticky sheets of webbing, muffling all sound.

The path begins to climb, and you leave the grasping mangrove roots behind for the treacherous slopes of Hollowshade Hill. A deep, unnatural twilight, cast by the colossal tree above, smothers the landscape. Every surface is coated in thick, sticky sheets of webbing, stretching between trees like grotesque sails and carpeting the forest floor in a silent, white blanket. The air is unnervingly still, the only sound the soft, disgusting tear of your boots pulling free from the webbed ground with every step. You feel the tingling sensation of being watched by countless, unseen eyes.

GM Notes

Stakes: This is a fight for survival. The environment is a weapon for the territorial predators that live here.

Environment Feature: Webbed Terrain. All Agility (Maneuver) rolls are made with disadvantage.

Environment Feature: Web Traps. If a character fails a movement-related roll with Fear, they become temporarily Restrained until they break free with a Strength (Smash) Roll (DC 14).

Dynamic Consequence Countdown (5): The Walls Close In. Advances when a PC is hit or restrained. At 0, a Webfall is triggered. All PCs must make an Agility Reaction Roll (DC 15). Failure: 2d10+2 physical damage & Restrained.

Adversaries:
  • 4x (begin Hidden)
  • 1x (arrives round 2)
Checks:
  • Instinct (Perceive) check DC 15: To spot the camouflaged Web Phantoms before the ambush.
Escalations (Fear Spends):
  • Spend 1 Fear: A Web Phantom uses `Web Walker` to phase through a web wall directly behind a PC and make an attack with advantage.
  • Spend 2 Fear: After a successful attack, a Web Phantom cocoons a PC, making them Restrained and pulling them 20 feet up into the canopy, isolating them.
